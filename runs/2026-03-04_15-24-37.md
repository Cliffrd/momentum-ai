## Analysis

**KEY_THEMES:**

- **Export functionality is a critical blocker** — 23 support tickets on row limits, 41 requests for CSV improvements, and Sarah's 2-hour weekly export ritual all point to exports being inadequate for real workflows
- **User management is confusing at every stage** — 31 tickets on "how to add a user," 38 requests for bulk management, Sarah's permissions chaos, and James discovering bulk actions by accident in week 3
- **Onboarding fails to surface core value** — James nearly churned because he didn't find bulk actions; the 22 requests for an onboarding checklist suggest users know they're missing things
- **HubSpot integration is the loudest single request** — 44 tickets + 67 feature requests makes this the most frequently requested item by volume; likely a wedge into a specific customer segment
- **Self-service documentation/discoverability is weak** — The volume of basic "how do I..." tickets (add user, audit log, bulk actions) suggests users can't find answers without contacting support

**OPPORTUNITIES:**

- **Scheduled/formatted export with unlimited rows** — Solves Sarah's 2-hour Monday pain and 23 tickets; likely unlocks expansion in enterprise segment — Effort: Medium
- **HubSpot integration** — Highest raw demand (111 combined mentions); likely blocking deals or causing churn in sales-led orgs — Effort: High
- **Guided onboarding with progressive feature disclosure** — Prevents "accidental churn" like James; reduces basic support tickets — Effort: Medium
- **Bulk user management + clearer permissions UI** — Addresses IT friction (Sarah) and 38 feature requests; table stakes for enterprise expansion — Effort: Medium
- **In-app contextual help / empty states** — Low-effort way to reduce 31 "how do I add a user" type tickets — Effort: Low

**HYPOTHESIS:**

"If we ship scheduled exports with custom formatting and no row limits, then enterprise admins like Sarah will reduce manual reporting time by 80%+, because they're currently exporting weekly to meet boss-mandated formats and hitting row caps that force workarounds."

**PRD_OUTLINE:**

**Problem Statement:**
Enterprise users spend hours weekly on manual exports because current functionality caps at 1,000 rows and doesn't support scheduling or output formatting. This creates churn risk and blocks expansion in accounts where reporting workflows are non-negotiable.

**Target User:**
Enterprise admins/ops leads who report to executives weekly/monthly (Sarah persona — 100+ seats, data-driven boss, limited IT support)

**Success Metrics:**
- Reduce export-related support tickets by 60% within 8 weeks of launch
- 25% of enterprise accounts adopt scheduled exports within 30 days
- NPS increase in enterprise segment (baseline → +10)

**Proposed Solution:**
- Remove 1,000 row export cap (or raise to 100k with async processing)
- Add scheduled exports (daily/weekly/monthly) delivered via email or cloud storage
- Provide template formatting options (match Sarah's "boss format" use case)

**Open Questions:**
- What's the infrastructure cost of large async exports? Need eng estimate.
- Do we gate this to paid tiers or use it as an upsell trigger?
- Should we combine this with a "saved views" feature so exports are repeatable?
